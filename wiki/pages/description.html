Description

Inspiration

Our feet, often the most neglected part of the body, are susceptible to fungal and bacterial infections due to the cosy, humid conditions in shoes. These infections cause itchy, flaky skin and foul odours, often ignored until they lead to serious secondary infections. Despite available antifungal and antibacterial treatments, they require frequent application and can be easily washed away. This is impractical for those with long working hours, such as office-goers, athletes, construction workers, and students, who cannot tend to their feet regularly. Existing fragrant products also pose environmental hazards. Inspired by these challenges, our project, E. solei, aims to provide a lasting, eco-friendly solution that keeps feet fresh, odour-free, and protected from infections without the need for constant reapplication.

Why we choose this?

Feet, the least cared-for part of our body, suffer significantly from fungal and bacterial skin diseases, causing substantial disability. In India, the non-fatal burden of these diseases surpasses that of cardiovascular diseases, with years lived with disability rising from 4.07 million in 1990 to 6.26 million in 2017. Students, professionals, athletes, firefighters, policemen, and soldiers face persistent foot care challenges due to prolonged shoe wear, leading to infections and foot odour. Soldiers in hot, humid conditions show high rates of bacterial (21.2%) and fungal (23.3%) infections. Despite existing solutions, a more effective, convenient, and long-lasting foot care solution is needed to prevent and treat these infections, especially for those unable to tend to their feet regularly. The rise in fungal infections, coupled with issues like prosthetic malfunctions and diabetes, highlights the urgent need for innovative solutions like E. solei.

PROOF OF CONCEPT

A proof of concept (POC) in design is a small exercise to test the design idea or assumption. It's about quickly creating a working prototype to demonstrate the feasibility of a design solution. POCs help in validating ideas, identifying potential issues early, and gaining stakeholder buy-in before investing significant resources into full-scale development.

USER EXPERIENCE (UX) DESIGN

UX design is about creating products that provide meaningful and relevant experiences to users. This field encompasses aspects of branding, design, usability, and function. UX designers focus on the entire process of acquiring and integrating a product, including aspects of branding, design, usability, and function.

USER INTERFACE (UI) DESIGN

UI design focuses on the look and feel, the presentation and interactivity of a product. It's all about making sure the user interface of a product is as intuitive as possible, and that means carefully considering each visual, interactive element the user might encounter. A good UI design ensures that the user interface is both efficient and enjoyable.

RESPONSIVE DESIGN

Responsive design is an approach to web design that makes web pages render well on a variety of devices and window or screen sizes. It uses CSS and HTML to resize, hide, shrink, enlarge, or move the content to make it look good on any screen. Responsive design has become increasingly important with the rise of mobile device usage.

ACCESSIBILITY DESIGN

Accessibility design ensures that products, services, and environments are usable by people with disabilities. This includes considerations for visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological disabilities. Good accessibility design benefits everyone by creating more inclusive and user-friendly experiences.

INTERACTION DESIGN

Interaction design is about creating engaging interfaces with well thought out behaviors. Understanding how users and technology communicate with each other is fundamental to this field. The goal of interaction design is to create products that enable the user to achieve their objective(s) in the best way possible.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Information architecture involves organizing, structuring, and labeling content in an effective and sustainable way. The goal is to help users find information and complete tasks. It's about creating a structure and nomenclature for a website, app, or other digital product that allows users to navigate it easily and intuitively.
